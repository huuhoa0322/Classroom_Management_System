Power/Interest Grid Analysis

Mapping the involvement of individuals and departments within the center that directly impact the system's development outcomes for the 3-week MVP.

Stakeholder / Role Power Interest Objectives & Expectations BA Management Strategy
Sponsor / Decider
Center Director 		High High Focuses on the big picture: reducing student churn rate by 20%, securing cash flow, and enhancing brand trust with strict project timelines (3-week MVP). Manage Closely: Provide rigorous weekly progress reports. Align closely on MVP scope priority and demonstrate automation metrics.
Key User
Academic Admin (Giáo vụ) 		Med High Needs to reclaim 15 man-hours/week. Desires a unified database, eliminating manual spreadsheets. Requires real-time system alerts 2 weeks prior to package depletion to process renewals. Keep Satisfied: Involve deeply in UX flows for data entry and scheduling. Ensure the transition from Excel to the system is smooth and painless.
End User
Teachers 		Low Med Must comply with the SLA to publish academic feedback within 12 hours post-session. Needs absolutely 0 scheduling conflicts (no double booking). Keep Informed / Satisfied: Design highly intuitive mobile-friendly interfaces for quick feedback submission. Guarantee foolproof room and schedule assignment.
External User
Parents (Sponsors) 		Low High Expects 100% automated, zero-touch communication. Needs real-time visibility into child's attendance and schedule changes via Email. Monitor: Ensure notification systems (Email dispatches) have near 100% deliverability. Validate message clarity and formatting.

📝 BA Note: Since parents rarely download native center apps, sticking to Push/Email notifications is crucial for MVP success to ensure high reachability. Additionally, maintaining zero scheduling conflicts is a strict algorithmic necessity to win Teachers' trust.
